Show Your Face is another project from the minor Visual Interface Design at the University of Applied Sciences Amsterdam. In this class we delved deeper into typography and made a site for a choosen font. In my case Frutiger.

Jump to site

Overview

Learning about typography and giving a font an identity.

For this project I first went out on the streets and took pictures of font's that spoke to me. For these series of photo's I had a theme in mind, Fonts used in public transport.


After collecting and analysing all photo's, I realised that most of them had one thing in common: Wheter is was the Amsterdam Metro System, the Rotterdam Metro System, my local bus or the NS; Most of them used the same font, It was either fruitger or an adaptation of frutiger.

About the project

After thoroughly researching Frutiger and his font, it was time to put my knowledge to work and create a website with fitting identity for the font, or well Show My Face.

Why Frutiger?

I've been a huge fan of public transport for the longest time, Especially trains. Which is why I picked it as my topic for the photos. Frutiger is used by a lot of instances for its great legibility while still having a open en inviting feel. It's a typeface originally made for the Charles de Gaulle

The website

For the website I wanted to created a very simple and clean look, Representing the usage of the face. For this I took elements usage in signage from Schiphol Airport (where they also used Frutiger) and the colors from Nessie, The design system from NS. Combining this resulted in a fun but a still very clean design showcasing the history and different aspects of the Frutiger Typeface.

The site

The site was made in Framer, A tool that I am trying to get more familiar with so I thought it would be a nice challenge to try it! It ended up working pretty well and the website is up and running. You can take a look at it throught the link below.
